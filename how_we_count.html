How We Count Insects Using Computer Vision 🧠📹🐜

We use a small camera — a Wyze Outdoor Cam — to record videos of the insects in your garden or park. Then, we use a special program written in Python to look through each frame of the video.

This program uses something called computer vision — it helps the computer “see” movement in videos, kind of like how your eyes notice something that suddenly moves.

Here’s how it works:

  • It first figures out what doesn't move — like dirt or leaves — and treats that as the background
  • Then, it watches for tiny changes — those are the insects moving across the scene!
  • The video is turned into black and white to make movement easier to spot
  • When it sees something moving, it draws green boxes around it to track it
  • It keeps counting how many insects it sees in each frame
  • At the end, it gives us the average number of insects that appeared in the video

🐜 It's like giving the computer a pair of digital bug-spotting goggles!
