Why Are Native Pollinators Important?

✅ Ecological Stability: Native pollinators have co-evolved with local plant species, ensuring effective and specialized pollination for those plants.

✅ Biodiversity Support: Native pollinators maintain healthy ecosystems by enabling plants to reproduce.

✅ Agricultural Productivity: Native bees like mason bees and bumblebees are often more effective than honeybees at pollinating crops.

✅ Soil and Water Health: By supporting diverse plant life, native pollinators contribute to healthier root systems.

✅ Food Web Support: Many birds, mammals, and insects rely on seeds and fruits that native pollinators help produce.

Why Are Native Pollinators in Decline?

1. Habitat Loss: Urbanization, deforestation, and land development reduce habitats where pollinators thrive.

2. Pesticide Use: Neonicotinoids and other chemicals impair pollinators' behavior and success.

3. Invasive Species: Non-native pollinators outcompete native species for resources.

4. Climate Change: Rising temperatures and unpredictable weather disrupt synchronized plant-pollinator relationships.

5. Disease and Parasites: Native bees are vulnerable to pathogens like Varroa mites and Nosema fungus.

6. Fragmentation of Resources: Monoculture farming reduces the diversity of plants available for pollinators.
